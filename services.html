Types Of Donation

The human body contains five litres of blood, made of several valuable components, i.e.Whole blood, Platelet, Plasma, and Red cells. Each component has several medical uses and can be used for different medical treatments. Your blood donation determines the best donation for you to make. You must have donated whole blood for plasma and platelet donation in the past two years.

Red Cells
what is it ?

Red blood cells are round with a flattish, indented centre, like doughnuts without a hole. Your healthcare provider can check on your red blood cells' size, shape, and health using a blood test.

Who can donate?

Anyone between 18 and 65 years of age and in normal health, with a bodyweight of 45 kg or more and a haemoglobin content of no less than 12.5 gms/hundred ml can be a donor. Up to 3 times/year

User For?

Transporting oxygen from your lungs to your body's tissues.

Plasma
what is it ?

The straw liquid in which red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets float in. It contains particular nutrients that can create 18 different types of medical products to treat many other medical conditions.

Who can donate?

You need to be 18-70(men) or 20-70 (women) years old, weigh 50kg or more, and must have given successful whole blood donation in the last two years.

User For?

Platets
what is it ?

The tiny 'plates' in blood wedge together to help clot and reduce bleeding. Always in high demand, Vital for people with low platelet count, like malaria and cancer patients.

Who can donate?

You need to be 18-70 years old(men), weigh 50kg or more, and have successfully donated Plasma in the past 12 months.

User For?

Cancer, blood diseases ,haemophilia, anaemia,heart disease ,stomach disease, kidney disease ,childbirth, operations ,blood loss, trauma ,burns.
